Project purpose

Why we built Campus Mood Diary

The game aims to help students see how everyday behaviors on campus are linked to psychological states, and to practice balancing study, rest, and social life in a low-risk environment.

Core serious goals

What the game is trying to teach.

  • ‚Ä¢ Make the connection between choices, consequences, and mood more visible.
  • ‚Ä¢ Encourage balanced decision-making between short-term comfort and long-term benefit.
  • ‚Ä¢ Support simple emotional self-regulation and reflection on campus life.

Who this is designed for.

Design

How the game works

The design focuses on short vignette-style scenes, simple choices, and clear stat feedback, implemented in Ren‚ÄôPy with a clean, minimal UI.

Core gameplay

Scenes, choices, and feedback.

  • Scenes
    Everyday CUHKSZ situations
    Players encounter familiar campus scenarios such as early-morning classes, overlapping deadlines, club activities, and group work.
  • Choices
    2‚Äì3 grounded options
    Each scene offers a few realistic choices that trade off immediate comfort (e.g., sleeping in) against long-term value (e.g., attending class or preparing in advance).
  • Feedback
    Five simple status bars
    Mood, confidence, anxiety, academic progress, and social interaction are shown as simple monochrome bars at the top of the screen, updating in real time as players make decisions.

Design choices

Simplified but grounded implementation.

  • ‚Ä¢ Built in Ren‚ÄôPy as a text-based visual novel for quick iteration.
  • ‚Ä¢ Kept a clean dark UI with straightforward stat bars instead of color-coding.
  • ‚Ä¢ Light background music and an end-of-day stats summary to reinforce reflection.
